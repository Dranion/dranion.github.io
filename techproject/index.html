Starting With Citizen Science

Whether you want to make a positive change in the world through research in your local communities, want to expand your own scientific ability, or simply explore the world, citizen science is a great thing to participate in. This guide will help you to begin your own experiments. If you already know what you're looking for, check the navbar above. Otherwise, keep reading!

Citizen Science is simply the collection of scientific information by regular people like you or me. It can be anything, from simply counting the birds in your backyard to testing the water quality of your local streams.

Deciding What Your Goal Is

It's important to know why you want to get into citizen science before you start purchasing products. If you know exactly what you want to get out of your experience, it's easier to decide what you want to put in.

Initial Interest
If you already have a specific experiment that you want to do, what you need to do next is choose your equipment. There's two ways to go about deciding what equipment to buy:

  1. Design your experiment from the equipment you can buy
  2. Design your experiment and then buy equipment

Designing your experiment from your equipment is a better choice if you have a limited budget . If you have a lot of resources, however, fully designing your ideal experiment and then purchasing the equipment is the way to get the best results.

If you're getting into Citizen Science because some new technology such as CRISPR caught your eye, you probably already know the equipment you're interested in buying. Looking at the possible experiments you can do based on the equipment you want is the best way to start. You may find that the really interesting experiments require more equipment or research!

If you're looking to find friends that are similary interested in science and the natural world, there's a lot of options with citizen science. Check out the community page for organizations and groups that have oppertunities to participate in Citizen Science.

Doing citizen science is a great way for both kids and adults to learn more about their surroundings, themselves, and science. Browse the experiments to see if anything catches your eye, and then look for some suitable equipment.
